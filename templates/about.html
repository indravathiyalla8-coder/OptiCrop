About OptiCrop System

Learn about the technology, methodology, and scope behind this smart farming system.

Project Objective

The primary goal of OptiCrop is to develop a scientific decision support application that assists farmers, agronomists, and agricultural planners in selecting the most profitable and high-yielding crops. By analyzing specific soil nutritional levels and macroclimatic conditions, the system minimizes human guesswork, reduces crop failures, and fosters sustainable cultivation practices.

Problem Statement

Traditional farming relies heavily on ancestral knowledge, which can be vulnerable to rapid climate changes, irregular rainfall patterns, and declining soil fertility. Planting the wrong crop leads to poor harvests, heavy economic losses for families, and soil degradation due to excessive chemical fertilizer usage. There is an urgent need for an interactive, data-driven system that evaluates environmental parameters and suggests optimal crops before planting begins.

Machine Learning Algorithm

OptiCrop implements a Random Forest Classifier, an ensemble machine learning algorithm that constructs a multitude of decision trees during training. It merges their predictions to yield a more stable and accurate prediction.

Why Random Forest? It handles non-linear relationships efficiently, avoids overfitting by averaging multiple decision trees, offers high feature robustness, and yields reliable probability estimates (used for the confidence score).

Dataset Profile

  • Source: Standard Precision Agriculture dataset.
  • Instances: 2,200 records representing crop conditions.
  • Features analyzed:
    • Nitrogen (N), Phosphorus (P), Potassium (K) in soil
    • Temperature (°C) and Relative Humidity (%)
    • Soil pH (acidity/alkalinity scale)
    • Rainfall (mm depth)
  • Classes: 22 diverse commercial food and cash crops.
